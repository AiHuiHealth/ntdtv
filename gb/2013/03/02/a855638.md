<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】非典十年 重大信息披露进步了吗?</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2013年03月02日讯】严重急性呼吸综合症（Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome，SARS）—— 萨斯，中国大陆称为“非典”。10年前，中国大陆有5327人感染“非典”、349人死亡。这场严重的公共卫生危机改变了许多人的命运。当年因揭露萨斯病而遭到中共当局软禁的蒋彦永医生也是其中一位。他在“SARS十周年纪念座谈会”上再度强调：真话的重要性。另外，大陆媒体刊登有关萨斯危机发生十周年的长篇报导。报导认为，当年萨斯病的发生，对中共政府重大信息披露起到了重大促进作用。然而有评论指出，表面上，中共是有一套重大讯息披露程序，但实际上还要看领导的意图行事。</p>
<p>28号，在北京局行的“SARS十周年纪念座谈会”，有20多名医学人员、萨斯病患和媒体人士出席。因揭露北京萨斯疫情而遭中共软禁的老军医蒋彦永，也出席座谈会。82岁高龄的他强调：讲真话最重要，讲真话并不困难，也不要怕讲错。</p>
<p>据报导，参与座谈的还有《中国青年报》图片总监贺延光，他回忆当年萨斯疫情爆发时的经过，他说，“2月就听说了，4月20号以后才能有报导。我们媒体太听话了！”他还表示，10年过去了，有些事还不能讲。</p>
<p>中国大陆网路作家荆楚：“西方的媒体，以揭露社会的丑陋阴暗的事情为职责。中国的媒体，就是报喜不报忧，丧事当成喜事办。老百姓生不如死了，他媒体上是到处莺歌燕舞，形势一片大好，不是小好，而是越来越好。这就起了一个蒙蔽、麻醉国人的作用。”</p>
<p>而大陆最新一期的《中国新闻周刊》，也刊登有关萨斯危机发生十周年的长篇报导。作者认为，当年萨斯病的发生，对中国政府重大信息披露起到了“重大促进作用，成为中国公共卫生史上的一个转捩点和里程碑。”</p>
<p>深圳独立学者朱建国在接受《自由亚洲电台》访问时表示，他本人并没有看到中共政府在重大信息披露方面，因为萨斯病而有所进步。 </p>
<p>深圳独立学者朱建国：“我看到今天有一条消息报了，广东有一个地方出现了口蹄疫。现在已经非常严重了，但是在事前，在苗头状态的时候一点消息都没有。就像当年的非典(即萨斯病)一样，最初他们(中共)也是没有披露，到最后已经不可隐瞒的时后，才披露。习近平虽然说政府要多听听批评，但是我们看到的实际：评论和批评的版面却越来越少。”</p>
<p>时政评论家蓝述则向《新唐人》表示，中共官员之间关系盘根错节，官员说真话，将牵一发而动全身。</p>
<p>时政评论家蓝述：“中共的官员上下互相之间，有一种提拔的关系，所以说这个关系盘根错节，牵一发而动全身，所以他要说真话的时候，他要考虑还会影响到谁，提拔他的人和被他提拔的人，所以他会考虑很大的一个问题，所以最后就是啥都不说了。”</p>
<p>蓝述还表示，所谓的讯息披露的进步，还是拜互联网所赐。</p>
<p>蓝述：“是因为互联网的迅速发展，所以有很多事情它（中共）瞒不住了，那瞒不住的话就只有公开了，那么，对于一些直接威胁到中共的政权稳定的重大的消息，它是绝对不报导的。”</p>
<p>朱建国也认为，在重大信息披露方面，中国只是制定了一批看上去很好看的披露程序，但实际上还要看领导的意图行事。</p>
<p>朱建国：“主要领导他开口了，你才能够弄（披露重大信息）。那个程序都是假的，是空的。这就像中国的宪法和法律不知道（制定）有多少，但是实际上呢？”</p>
<p>中国工程院院士钟南山，也是抗击萨斯的旗帜性人物。日前他在接受媒体采访时也表示，目前应对突发公共卫生事件，最突出的问题就是疾病信息的覆盖和上报。他说：“个别地区上报的病例数和死亡人数不够准确，这与某些地方官员担心这些数字会影响自己的政绩有关。”</p>
<p>采访/刘惠编辑/黄亿美后制/郭敬</p>
<p>Any Progress on Information Disclosure?</p>
<p>Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome, (SARS), was called<br />‘atypical pneumonia’ in mainland China.<br />10 years ago, (Chinese media) reported 5,327 people infected<br />with SARS in mainland China and 349 people died.<br />This serious public health crisis has changed the<br />fate of many people.<br />Dr. Jiang Yanyong, who exposed the SARS epidemic, was under<br />house arrest by the Chinese Communist Party (CCP).<br />He once again stressed during the SARS 10th anniversary<br />symposium the importance of telling the truth.<br />In addition, the mainland media published lengthy reports<br />about the SARS epidemic tenth anniversary. <br />The report said that when SARS occurred, it played a significant<br />role in promoting disclosure of major information to the CCP.<br />However, it was said that, on the surface, the CCP has a set of<br />information disclosure procedures.<br />In reality it acts according to the leadership&#8217;s intent.</p>
<p>On February 28th the SARS 10th anniversary symposium<br />was held in Beijing.<br />More than 20 medical personnel, SARS patients,<br />and the media were invited to attend. <br />Jiang Yanyong was also invited under house arrest due to<br />exposing SARS. <br />Jiang, aged 82, stressed that “It is most important to tell the truth,<br />but not difficult, do not be afraid to say anything wrong.”</p>
<p>According to reports, He Yanguang, the director of photos<br />of the China Youth Daily also attended.<br />He recalled the process of the outbreak of the SARS epidemic.</p>
<p>He said," We heard in February, but were only allowed<br />to report on April 20th. We media were too obedient,"<br />He also said that, 10 years later, some things still cannot <br />be spoken out loud.</p>
<p>Internet writer Jing Chu: “Western media duties<br />are to expose the ugly dark things in the society.<br />But media in China reported nothing but good news,<br />and even handle a funeral as a happy event.<br />The people felt better to die rather than to live. </p>
<p>The (CCP) media were full of the joy of spring saying the<br />situation is excellent, not trivial, but getting better and better.<br />This played a role to blind and anesthetize the people."</p>
<p>The latest issue of Newsweek in mainland China also published<br />lengthy reports about the SARS crisis tenth anniversary.<br />The authors suggested that when SARS occurred, it played a<br />“significant role in promoting disclosure of information to the CCP,<br />becoming a turning point and milestone in the history<br />of public health.”</p>
<p>Independent scholar Zhu Jianguo in Shenzhen<br />was interviewed by Radio Free Asia.<br />He said he did not see any progress of the CCP in the<br />disclosure of major information because of SARS.</p>
<p>Zhu Jianguo: &#8220;I read in the newspaper that in Guangdong<br />there were foot-and-mouth disease outbreaks. <br />Although it was reported, the epidemic is now very serious.</p>
<p>There was no information disclosure of the epidemic when<br />it just appeared. Just as SARS, it was not disclosed initially.<br />When the development of the epidemic couldn’t be concealed,<br />the CCP only then disclosed it to the public. <br />Xi Jinping said the Government should listen more to criticism,<br />actually see the forums of comment and criticism become fewer."</p>
<p>Political critic Lan Shu told NTD television that there are deep-<br />rooted relations between the CCP officials; <br />if officials spoke the truth, it will indeed affect them all.</p>
<p>Lan Shu: &#8220;There is a promotion relationship between<br />the CCP officials of different levels.<br />This makes the relationship become deeply rooted.<br />It indeed affects the whole body, if one tells the truth.<br />So he will have to consider whom will be affected among<br />those who promoted him and whom he promoted.<br />He will be considered a big problem,<br />so consequently he said nothing in the end."</p>
<p>Lan Shu also said that the so-called progress of message<br />disclosure is thanks to the Internet.</p>
<p>Lan Shu: “Since the internet developed, a lot of things could<br />not be concealed by the (CCP), then it had to disclose them.<br />But for some news having a direct threat to the stability of<br />the CCP regime, it is will never report it.”(Voice from RFA)</p>
<p>Zhu Jianguo also believes that in the disclosure of<br />major information,<br />China just developed a disclosure program which <br />looks very nice, but depends on leadership intending to act.</p>
<p>Zhu Jianguo: “If principal leaders did not allow it,<br />nobody dared to disclose major information. <br />The information disclosure procedures developed are <br />false and inane.<br />It&#8217;s like the Chinese Constitution and relevant laws<br />are enacted well, but how they were actually carried out?"</p>
<p>Zhong Nanshan, an academic of China Academy of Engineering,<br />was also a public figure in the movement against SARS.<br />In a recent media interview, he also said currently to respond to<br />public health emergencies, the most prominent problem is the coverage and reporting of disease information.<br />He said, the reported number of cases and deaths<br />in some regions were not accurate enough.<br />“It is related to some local officials worried that these figures<br />will affect their own achievements.”</p>
