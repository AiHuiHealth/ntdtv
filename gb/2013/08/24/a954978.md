<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】危险的中国水坝  或是水灾真凶</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2013年08月24日讯】中国是世界上拥有水库大坝数量最多的国家，但<ahref="https://github.com/huihjg380/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/溃坝.md#1">溃坝</a>率也居世界前列，一项调研结果表明，中国近9万座水库大坝，基本都已年久失修。其中有四万多座<ahref="https://github.com/huihjg380/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/水坝.md#1">水坝</a>“带病”运转，使得中国四分之一的县市随时面临<ahref="https://github.com/huihjg380/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/溃坝.md#1">溃坝</a>后被冲毁的危险。有专家还指出，造成今年南北方洪水肆虐的主要原因，很可能与这些“病险水库”的溃坝有关。</p>
<p>连日来，大陆各地不断传出暴雨袭击后，冲毁堤坝造成洪灾肆虐伤亡惨重的消息，使得各界再次将目光聚焦到备受争议的中国水库大坝。</p>
<p>中国地质学家杨勇：“中国<ahref="https://github.com/huihjg380/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/水坝.md#1">水坝</a>的问题，实际上这几年大家都在谈论，官方也有定论。中国水坝达到了将近10万座，大大小小的，其中60%左右，属于‘病险库’。在2012的时候，国家水利部曾经发布了这些信息。”</p>
<p>据中共水利部普查结果显示，中国的水库大坝由于工程质量差、年代久、设施老化加上缺乏必须的维护，目前至少有4万多座水库存在病险，随时面临溃坝，直接威胁到了179个城市和285座县城。</p>
<p>杨勇：“从中国这么大的水库的数量，它的病险率占60%，这个量是相当大的，这些病险库在极端气候的状况下，肯定出现溃坝的概率是相当大的，它所带来的损失和破坏力也是相当大的。”</p>
<p>有数据显示，中国大坝数量居世界首位，年均溃坝率是世界年均溃坝率的4倍多。中共水利部部长在接受媒体采访时也曾表示，截至2005年底，中国每年平均有68座水库垮坝。而几乎所有的水库都存在坝体渗水、坝身薄弱等诸多安全隐患。因此每到汛期，小型水库出现险情、溃坝的事故时有发生。</p>
<p>杨勇：“从今年南方和北方的<ahref="https://github.com/huihjg380/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/水灾.md#1">水灾</a>的情况来看，可能都和这些水坝有关系。但是到现在为止，水灾的原因还没有一个明确说法。除了气候降雨的原因以外，是不是和水坝相关，到现在为止，我们还没看到有关方面的结论，但是从水灾的状况来看呢，很多城市被淹没、很多村镇被淹没，很多江河的防洪压力巨大，势必和一些病险水库的溃坝有关系。”</p>
<p>据报导，在今年的南北大洪灾中，广东普宁练江多处溃坝，海水倒灌，导致多人死亡﹔汕头市潮南地区大堤水库多处堤防崩塌，淹水高达3米，大批民众受困。而1975年河南的板桥水库溃坝，据传更是造成了24万人丧生洪水的悲剧。</p>
<p>对此民众质问当局，堤坝为什么会崩溃，结构问题？材料组成问题？设计问题？还是豆腐渣工程？为什么在洪期到来之前不事先加固和维修堤坝？对此，当局的答复为：经费奇缺，无力维修。</p>
<p>而另一方面，中共官员利用整修水库进行贪污腐败的案例却不断被曝光。有媒体报导，2005年6月，因官员挪用整修水库预算购买名车，导致黑龙江沙兰镇年久失修的水库不堪豪雨而溃堤，洪水瞬间淹没位于下游的中心小学，造成105名小学生死亡。</p>
<p>而中共水利部1988年就已经在账上的基建投资预算1.2亿元，本来是应该安排给项目单位应急使用的，但直到2000年，这笔款项依然闲置。</p>
<p>采访编辑/张天宇 后制/李智远</p>
<p>Sick Dams: Real Reason Behind China&#8217;s Fatal Flooding </p>
<p>China has the most reservoir dams in the world,<br />and the highest dam failure rate. <br />China has built nearly 90,000 dams, whilst nearly<br />all of them have been under of repair for years.<br />Over 40,000 in-service dams in China are “sick”.</p>
<p>This has put 25% of counties at risk of destruction<br />after a dam breach.<br />Experts think that the reason behind this year&#8217;s<br />flooding in the North and South, <br />was more likely caused by the<br />collapse of these “sick” dams.</p>
<p>For days, China&#8217;s media have been reporting on fatal floods,<br />and dam breaking due to heavy rainfall. <br />Global attention was once again given to <br />China&#8217;s reservoir dams, a controversial issue.</p>
<p>Chinese geologist, Yang Yong: “China&#8217;s dam issue<br />has been talked about for years. <br />The authorities have reached a final conclusion.</p>
<p>China has almost 100,000 dams, 60% of them<br />are in a dangerous state. <br />This was the information released by the<br />Ministry of Water Resources in 2012.”</p>
<p>An official survey shows the problems with China&#8217;s dams.</p>
<p>They are of inferior quality, with long-term services,<br />facility decaying, and lack of adequate maintenance.<br />Over 40,000 reservoirs in China are at risk of a breach.</p>
<p>They are posing a direct threat to the safety of<br />179 cities and 285 counties.</p>
<p>Yang Yong: “It is a very high rate with 60%<br />being classified as being dangerous reservoirs.<br />In extreme weather conditions, it is highly<br />probable of a dam break. <br />That will cause large loss and destruction.”</p>
<p>Public data show that China has the most dams in the world.</p>
<p>The annual average rate of a dam break is<br />four times that of the world norm. <br />In 2006, China&#8217;s Water Resources vice minister admitted<br />that by 2005, China had an average of 68 dam breaches yearly.<br />Dam seepage and weakness are common problems<br />with many reservoirs.<br />During the flood season, dangerous situations often<br />occur in small reservoirs, which include dam failure.</p>
<p>Yang Yong: “This year&#8217;s flooding in the North and South <br />were all affected by these dams. <br />But so far, the authorities have <br />given no explicit explanations. <br />In addition to rainfall, whether the floods are related<br />to dams? We haven&#8217;t seen any official conclusion yet.<br />Now many cities and towns have been flooded, and<br />river flood control systems are facing huge pressure.<br />I think there is a link between the flooding and<br />breaching of some sick dams.”</p>
<p>Reportedly, in Puning, Guangdong Province, <br />dam breaks have caused many deaths this year.<br />A Dam breach in Shantou had caused a three-meter flood level,<br />which trapped a large number of local residents.<br />Back in 1975, the Banqiao reservoir dam failure occurred<br />in Henan Province, leading to 240,000 deaths.</p>
<p>The public has now raised their queries——<br />why did the dams collapse? <br />Were there problems with structure, material composition,<br />designing, or were they shoddy constructions? <br />People are asking why no prior reinforcing and repairs<br />were applied to these dams in the pre-flood period?<br />The authorities&#8217; reply was:a critical shortage of funds,<br />which leads to non maintenance.</p>
<p>However, corruption of officials involved in reservoir<br />maintenance have been continuously exposed.<br />Reportedly, in Shatian, a town of Helongjiang Province,<br />officials spent the budget for reservoir renovations to buy famous cars.<br />In June 2005, the dam collapsed due to heavy rains.</p>
<p>The flood drowned a primary school located downstream,<br />claiming the lives of 105 students.</p>
<p>Media reports claim that China&#8217;s Water Resources Ministry<br />allocated a 120-million-yuan contingency fund <br />for local infrastructure investments in 1988.<br />Yet the fund remained idle until the year 2000.</p>
