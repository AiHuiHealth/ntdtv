<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>【禁闻】梁谈清场 学联不愿退 北京如何做？</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人2014年12月09日讯】香港<ahref="https://github.com/eirdkz3742/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/占中运动.md#1">占中运动</a>持续至今已有70多天，特首梁振英日前表示，会在适当时候宣布金钟占领区清场日期，并指占领后期，参与的人越少，行动会越激烈。对此，学联回应，愈来愈激烈的是警方。面对不见结束迹象的占中运动，中共当局会采取什么策略呢？我们一起来看报导。</p>
<p>香港特首梁振英7号考察深圳前海后表示，当局已经有心理及行动上的准备，占领行动到了后期，人数越少，留下来的参与者会越激烈，警方在清场或协助执达主任执行法院命令时，可能会遇到激烈抵抗。梁振英要求金钟和铜锣湾的占领人士，尤其是年轻人和学生，尽快撤离。</p>
<p>针对学民思潮成员绝食要求重启政改的诉求，梁振英表示，绝食及占中的学生不清楚《基本法》及人大决定，他还说，政改工作从没有停过，“不存在”重启政改五部曲。</p>
<p>学联副秘书长岑敖晖则回应，使用武力愈来愈激烈的是警方。他们与占领者商讨后，并没有打算现阶段撤离。岑敖晖还表示，即使占领结束，学生也会继续抗争，包括在政改方案出炉时向政府及立法会议员施加更大压力。</p>
<p>据了解，警方已作好准备，会在这个星期协助执达吏在中环执行禁制令。有占中人士向香港《明报》透露，将成立一个“勇武派”组织，以盾牌等守住防线。</p>
<p>英国《金融时报》指出，面对行动逐步升级的示威者，北京“等待抗议结束”的策略或许不再站得住脚。它必须要么作出让步，要么采取果断行动以结束抗议活动。但是，这两个选择，哪个也不容易。</p>
<p>旅美中国社会问题研究人士张健：“对待占中，让它是非常棘手的问题，第一不能用军队武力镇压，会导致国际社会可能对中国（共）进行全面的制裁，它现在是内忧外患，在经济上急需大量的订单，它也不能对香港普选有真正的回应，因为香港一旦实行真正普选，有可能全中国所有地方都会要求进行真正的普选，中共无法开这个口子。”</p>
<p>北京时政观察人士华颇：“我想，中共在等时机，它已经看到了香港占中者发生了一些错误，比如冲击立法会、冲击特首总部，中共在找一种镇压借口，只要占中者继续犯错，被中共抓住把柄了，它就会让国际社会看，它就会以暴制暴。但说是不是像六四那样真枪实弹，我看不大可能。”</p>
<p>《金融时报》报导指出，香港这场危机是对中共国家主席习近平领导能力的首次真正考验。在“一国两制”所提供的法律保护下，坚定、理想主义的香港年轻抗议者不会轻易被吓倒。</p>
<p>张健：“香港毕竟是被英国统治这么多年，每一个人都具备民主价值的观念，中共又号称依法治国，号称有基本法在前面，中共又被自己的东西所禁锢住了，这就是现在产生这样一个非常胶着的原因之一。”</p>
<p>旅美中国社会问题研究人士张健指出，香港争民主运动是中共江派刻意挑起的。</p>
<p>张健：“要把习近平拖下水，唯一的方式，现在就是用香港的问题。在一国两制上，在普选上，因为在真普选的问题上，在香港历年的游行活动中，中共的情治部门还是判断非常准确的，一旦把真普选这个问题给搁置之后，香港市民的反弹势必是非常大的，所以用这个方式，有可能会逼迫现政权和江派进行一种内部的交易。”</p>
<p>张健认为，<ahref="https://github.com/eirdkz3742/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/占中运动.md#1">占中运动</a>如果撤退，香港就没有未来。</p>
<p>张健：“如果香港人民退缩了，无论是中共的哪一派都会胜利，首先是中共的胜利，所以香港人民一定要去坚持，才会有希望。”</p>
<p>据报导，香港“伞下爸妈”等10几个团体，7号发起反警察暴力游行。此外，有市民自发举行28小时接力绝食行动，在学民的绝食区外搭起帐篷。不过，学民思潮召集人黄之锋表示，医疗人手不足，不建议学民以外成员加入绝食。 </p>
<p>采访/朱智善 编辑/陈洁</p>
<p>Federation of Students Object to Leung Chun-ying&#8217;s Call for Retreat <br />How Will Beijing React?</p>
<p>Hong Kong Occupy Central Movement has lasted <br />over 70 days. <br />Chief Executive Leung Chun-ying said that he would soon <br />announce the date for clearing the occupied Admiralty area. <br />He pointed out that in the later stage of the occupation, <br />the protesters would be fewer, but action would be more violent. <br />In this regard, the Federation of Students responded <br />that it&#8217;s the police that would be more and more violent. <br />Faced with the unending Occupy Central Movement, <br />what strategy will the CCP take? <br />Please take a look at the following reports.</p>
<p>Hong Kong Chief Executive Leung Chun-ying said <br />on Dec. 7, before his trip to Shenzhen, that the authorities <br />are in psychological and physical readiness. </p>
<p>That is, in the later stage of the occupation, the remaining <br />protesters would be fewer, but they might be more and more <br />violent, and when the police clear the occupied area or help <br />the chief bailiff to carry out the court order, they may <br />encounter fierce resistance. </p>
<p>Leung thus called on those who participate in the occupation <br />movement at the Admiralty and Causeway Bay, especially <br />youngsters and students, to evacuate the areas as soon as possible.</p>
<p>As to the fact that Scholarism members use hunger strike to <br />petition for restarting political reform, Mr. Leung said those <br />who go on hunger strike and the students participating <br />in the Occupy Central Movement do not know the &#8220;Basic Law" <br />and the decision made by the National People&#8217;s Congress. </p>
<p>He added that political reform has never been stopped. </p>
<p>So, there&#8217;s no need to have the so-called five steps <br />to restarting political reform.</p>
<p>Deputy Secretary General of the Federation Lester Shum <br />responded that it is the police that use weapons <br />more and more violently. </p>
<p>After consulting with those who participate in the Occupy <br />Central Movement, they have no plan to evacuate the place <br />at this stage. </p>
<p>Lester Shum also said that even if the occupation ended, <br />the students will continue to protest, including putting more <br />pressure on the government and the Legislative Council <br />after the political reform program is released.</p>
<p>It is understood that the police are poised to assist the bailiff <br />carry out the injunction at the Central this week. <br />Some Occupy Central protesters told Ming Pao Daily <br />that they would set up a &#8220;chivalrous faction" organization <br />to secure the beachhead with shields and other stuff.</p>
<p>Financial Times pointed out that faced with the protesters <br />who have escalated their action, Beijing&#8217;s tactic of &#8220;waiting <br />for an end to the protest" may no longer be tenable. </p>
<p>It must either make concessions or take decisive action <br />to end the protest. However, neither one would be an easy task.</p>
<p>US-based China&#8217;s social problems researcher Zhang Jian: <br />&#8220;Concession is a very tough issue. <br />For one thing, it cannot use military force to suppress the protesters. <br />It may lead to the international community&#8217;s comprehensive <br />sanctions against the Chinese regime. </p>
<p>It has encountered difficulties at home and abroad for the time <br />being. On the economic front, it needs a lot of orders badly. <br />For another, it cannot have a real response to the demand <br />for universal suffrage in Hong Kong, as once universal suffrage <br />is granted in Hong Kong, various places across China <br />are likely to demand genuine universal suffrage too. <br />As a result, the Chinese regime cannot open this door."</p>
<p>Beijing politics observer Hua Po: &#8220;I think, the Chinese regime <br />is waiting for the timing. <br />It has noticed some mistakes made by Hong Kong Occupy <br />Central protesters, such as the attacks on the Legislative Council <br />and the Chief Executive Headquarters. </p>
<p>The Chinese regime is looking for a pretext for repression. </p>
<p>As long as Occupy Central protesters continue to make mistakes, <br />the regime may grasp the opportunity to let the international <br />community know that violence must be met by violence. </p>
<p>But if some say that it may be something like the June-4th, <br />I think it&#8217;s unlikely."</p>
<p>The Financial Times reported that the crisis in Hong Kong <br />is the first real test for Chinese President Xi Jinping&#8217;s leadership. <br />Under the legal protection of &#8220;One Country, Two System," <br />the firm and idealistic young protesters in Hong Kong <br />would never be intimidated easily.</p>
<p>Zhang Jian: &#8220;After all, as Hong Kong was under British rule <br />for so many years, everyone is accustomed to the concept <br />of democratic values. </p>
<p>Moreover, since the Chinese regime has also bragged about <br />its rule of law, under the Basic Law, it has been entangled <br />by something of its own creation. </p>
<p>This is one of the reasons why it’s so stalemated."</p>
<p>Zhang Jian pointed out that the democratic movement <br />in Hong Kong was deliberately instigated by the Jiang faction.</p>
<p>Zhang Jian: &#8220;To drag Xi Jinping into trouble, the only way now <br />is to use the crisis in Hong Kong. <br />In terms of the &#8216;One Country, Two System&#8217; and universal suffrage, <br />in the marching activities in Hong Kong on the issue of genuine <br />universal suffrage in the past, the CCP intelligence agency&#8217;s<br />judgment was quite good. <br />Once the problem with genuine universal suffrage is put off, <br />Hong Kong people&#8217;s reaction would be very strong. <br />So, this approach may force the ruling regime to have <br />an internal deal with the Jiang faction."</p>
<p>Zhang Jian believes retreat will only bring an end <br />to the future of Hong Kong.</p>
<p>Zhang Jian: &#8220;If Hong Kong people retreat, either faction <br />within the CCP would be the winner. <br />So, Hong Kong people must stand firm, so that it may succeed. <br />If they lose the streets, they would lose Hong Kong&#8217;s future."</p>
<p>It is reported that over ten organizations in Hong Kong, <br />including the Umbrella Parents, <br />launched an anti-police brutality march on Dec. 7. </p>
<p>In addition, some residents voluntarily participated in the <br />28-hour hunger strike relay campaign in the tents outside <br />the hunger strike area occupied by Scholarism. </p>
<p>However, Scholarism student leader Joshua Wong said <br />that due to the shortage of medical personnel, <br />people other than Scholarism members are not encouraged <br />to join the hunger strike. </p>
