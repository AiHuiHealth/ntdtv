<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>照吃照喝不上健身房 美国胖妞年减逾50公斤（组图）</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.ntdtv.com/assets/uploads/2020/08/2020-08-06_151510-800x450.jpg" />
<h6>美国加州女子特威瑟尔曼（Kiah Twisselman）减重有成。</h6>
<hr>
	<p>【新唐人北京时间2020年08月06日讯】美国加州一名曾经重达100多公斤的女子在大约一年的期间里减重超过120磅（54公斤），而且没有上<ahref="https://github.com/bxhnqj353/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/健身房.md#1">健身房</a>，也没有刻意让自己饿肚子。她不但甩掉了身上的肥肉，也减轻了多年来心理上所承受的压力。那么，她是如何办到的呢？</p>
<p>这名在牧场工作的27岁女子特威瑟尔曼（Kiah Twisselman）从小就为肥胖所苦，她的<ahref="https://github.com/bxhnqj353/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/体重.md#1">体重</a>一度高达285磅（129公斤）。在看了《女孩洗把脸》（Girl Wash Your Face）这本书之后，她下决心要<ahref="https://github.com/bxhnqj353/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/减肥.md#1">减肥</a>。</p>
<p>有别于贸然采取节食等比较激进的<ahref="https://github.com/bxhnqj353/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/减肥.md#1">减肥</a>方法，特威瑟尔曼以逐步改变<ahref="https://github.com/bxhnqj353/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/生活方式.md#1">生活方式</a>来减轻<ahref="https://github.com/bxhnqj353/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/体重.md#1">体重</a>。她并没有上<ahref="https://github.com/bxhnqj353/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/健身房.md#1">健身房</a>运动，而且照样吃她喜爱的<ahref="https://github.com/bxhnqj353/ntdtv/blob/master/gb/食物.md#1">食物</a>，例如牛肉。<br />

<p style=" margin:8px 0 0 0; padding:0 4px;"> <ahref="https://www.instagram.com/p/CDWxii_H4QJ/?utm_source=ig_embed&amp;utm_campaign=loading" style=" color:#000; font-family:Arial,sans-serif; font-size:14px; font-style:normal; font-weight:normal; line-height:17px; text-decoration:none; word-wrap:break-word;" target="_blank">Going out to eat while trying to lose weight used to give me anxiety. Shortly after starting my health journey, we were invited to a birthday dinner for a friend at a local pizza joint. Before dinner I did what I still do to this day, I planned ahead. I looked up the menu and decided I was going to order a salad for dinner that night. Decision made. As we sat down and started visiting with our friends, the waitress came to take orders. Every single appetizer that was ordered for the table was bread-based. Hot fresh breadsticks are my kryptonite &#8211; I LOVE THEM. While everyone else was enjoying each other’s company, I was quietly and internally wallowing in my own self pity. I sat there letting my mind spiral, thinking things like, “I wish I could just eat foods like a normal person. I wish I didn’t have to follow these stupid diet rules. I don’t get to enjoy this birthday dinner as much as everyone else.” I honored my commitment, passing on the appetizers and ordering a salad for dinner. And guess what… I survived! It wasn’t until afterwards that I had a realization. Instead of letting my mind focus on thoughts that victimized myself, a more helpful thought could have been, “I am making an empowered choice to honor my decision, not because I CAN’T have bread, but because my long term goals are more important to me than the instant gratification of fresh breadsticks.” WOW. Same circumstance. Different thought. Entirely different feeling. The first thought made me feel like crap. The second thought made me feel proud that I was honoring a commitment to myself! My approach to dieting is that I CAN have whatever I want, but I get to make EMPOWERED CHOICES to plan on how to fuel my body best with balance and moderation. Some moments it just takes reminding myself of that, taking total ownership, not only of my actions, but also of my thoughts that influence them. I’ve said it once, and I’ll say it again &#8211; the most important and powerful part of this whole health journey is our mindset. You are not a victim to your circumstances. You are not a victim to diet rules. This is your life, take ownership of it and start making EMPOWERED CHOICES on how to live it.</a></p>
